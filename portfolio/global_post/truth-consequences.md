The opposition-led demonstrations demanded the dismantling of the
state-controlled media firm and the public media director be fired.
Protest organizer and opposition figure Peter Magyar criticized the
government-run media for its lies and propaganda, calling it "a global

Demonstrators also called for the restoration of genuine public media,
legal prohibitions on government propaganda and the resignation of news
directors accused of falsifying information.

[noted](https://post.spmailtechnol.com/f/a/i_PRavHHc-SjXLDEdLqrJQ~~/AASU4wA~/RgRo5jPTP0RCaHR0cHM6Ly9hLnBvc3QubGhkLmxpbmsvMTc0dHgxcnBwci8_bGhfYWlkPTExNDUwJmxoX2NpZD05ZjRlYmh6cDU4VwNzcGNCCmb41a4DZxHdTlxSGmFudGhpLmxpa2l0c2Frb3NAZ21haWwuY29tWAQAAABa).

TISZA secured nearly 30 percent of the vote in the European Parliament
